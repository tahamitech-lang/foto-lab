Step 2 — Body Type

DSLR vs Mirrorless

Factor DSLR Mirrorless
Battery life Better — optical viewfinder uses no power Shorter — EVF drains battery faster
Autofocus speed Good, but limited in video Faster, better eye-tracking
Size & weight Bulkier body and lenses Lighter and more compact
Lens selection Decades of used/affordable glass Newer mounts, growing fast
Future-proofing Canon & Nikon have moved to mirrorless Where the industry is investing now

If you're buying your first serious camera in 2026, we generally point people toward mirrorless — but if you already own EF or F-mount lenses, a DSLR body can still make sense to avoid re-buying glass.

Step 2 — Prime or Zoom?

Prime vs Zoom Lenses

Factor Prime Lens Zoom Lens
Aperture / low light Wider apertures (f/1.2 - f/1.8), better in dark venues Usually limited to f/2.8 at best
Sharpness Generally sharper at any given aperture Very good but rarely matches a prime
Flexibility Fixed focal length — you move your feet One lens covers many framings

Step 3 — Compatibility

Check Your Mount First

Lenses are mount-specific. Canon RF lenses won't fit an EF body without an adapter, and Sony E-mount lenses don't fit Nikon Z cameras at all. Always confirm your camera's mount before buying — our staff checks this for free in-store.

Common Questions

Quick Answers

Do I need an L-series / GM / S-line lens?

Only if you're shooting professionally or need weather sealing. Standard consumer lenses are sharp enough for most hobbyists and even paid work.

Is image stabilization (IS/VR/OSS) worth it?

Yes if you shoot handheld in low light or video. Less critical if your body already has in-body stabilization (IBIS).

Should I buy third-party lenses?

Sigma and Tamron now make excellent native-mount lenses at lower prices — a smart way to save without sacrificing much quality.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common Photography Questions

What camera should I buy for travel?

Look for a lightweight mirrorless body with a compact zoom lens. Fujifilm X-T50, Sony A6700, or Canon R50 are great options. Weather sealing is a bonus if you shoot outdoors often.

How many megapixels do I need?

24MP is plenty for most uses — you can print large and crop reasonably. 45MP+ is only necessary if you're doing professional studio work or need massive crops.

Is full-frame better than APS-C?

Full-frame gives better low-light performance and shallower depth of field. APS-C is lighter, cheaper, and lenses are more affordable. Both can produce professional results.

How do I clean my camera sensor?

Use a blower first to remove dust particles. If that doesn't work, use a sensor cleaning kit with swabs designed for your sensor size. Bring it to us if you're unsure — we offer professional cleaning services.

What's the best lens for beginners?

A 50mm f/1.8 prime is affordable, sharp, and teaches you to compose. A 24-70mm f/4 zoom is more versatile for everyday use. Both are excellent starting points.

How long do camera batteries last?

DSLR batteries typically last 800-1200 shots per charge. Mirrorless batteries last 300-600 shots depending on EVF usage. Always carry a spare for important shoots.
