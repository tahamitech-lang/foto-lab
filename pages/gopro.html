About the Brand

The Original Action Camera

GoPro was founded in 2002 by Nick Woodman, a surfer and entrepreneur who wanted a camera that could capture the thrill of action sports from a first-person perspective.

From its humble beginnings with 35mm film cameras strapped to surfboards, GoPro has grown into a global phenomenon with over 50 million cameras sold. Today, GoPro is the undisputed leader in action cameras, known for rugged durability, stunning image quality, and innovative features like HyperSmooth stabilization and 360-degree capture.

Brand Timeline

2002
GoPro founded by Nick Woodman in California. First camera — a 35mm film camera strapped to a surfboard.
2004
GoPro launches its first digital camera — the Hero, capable of capturing 10MP stills.
2010
GoPro Hero HD — the first action camera with 1080p video, revolutionizing the industry.
2014
GoPro Hero4 — introduces 4K video and HDR photo capabilities to the action camera world.
2019
GoPro Hero8 Black — HyperSmooth 2.0 stabilization sets a new standard for action camera steadiness.
2024
GoPro Hero13 Black — 5.3K60 video, HyperSmooth 6.0, and waterproof to 33 feet. The ultimate action camera.

Why Choose GoPro

Built for Adventure

Waterproof Design

GoPro cameras are waterproof up to 33 feet without a housing, making them perfect for surfing, diving, and all water sports.

HyperSmooth 6.0

Industry-leading digital image stabilization that makes handheld footage look like it was shot on a gimbal — smooth as silk.

Rugged Durability

Built to withstand drops, dust, and extreme temperatures. GoPro cameras are designed to survive the toughest adventures.

360° Capture

GoPro Max offers 360-degree capture, allowing you to reframe shots after the action — the ultimate immersive experience.
